Kerala, known as "Gods Own Country," is a state on the southwestern Malabar Coast of India. It was formed on 1 November 1956 following the States Reorganisation Act by combining Malayalam-speaking regions. Spread over 38,863 kmÂ², Kerala is the twenty-first largest Indian state by area. With 33 million inhabitants as per the 2011 census, Kerala is the thirteenth-largest Indian state by population. It is bordered by Karnataka to the north and northeast, Tamil Nadu to the east and south, and the Lakshadweep Sea to the west. The state's capital is Thiruvananthapuram, and its largest city is Kochi. Malayalam is the most widely spoken language and is also the official language of the state. Kerala has the lowest positive population growth rate in India, 3.44%; the highest Human Development Index (HDI), 0.784 in 2018; the highest literacy rate, 96.2% in the 2011 census; the highest life expectancy, 77.1 years; and the highest sex ratio, 1,084 women per 1,000 men. The state has witnessed significant emigration, especially to the Arab states of the Persian Gulf during the Gulf Boom of the 1970s and early 1980s, and is a major source of remittances to the country. Kerala is one of the prominent tourist destinations of India, with its hill stations, backwaters, beaches, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries. The unique culture and traditions of Kerala, coupled with its varied demography, have made Kerala one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world. The state also has a rich history of Ayurveda and traditional Indian healthcare practices. The art and architecture of Kerala are also remarkable with the state housing several ancient temples and monuments. Kerala has a large number of festivals celebrated with much enthusiasm and gaiety.The state is also known for its cuisine, which is characterized by the use of coconut and spices, making it distinct and flavorful. Kerala is home to a variety of wildlife and bird species, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. With a well-developed infrastructure, the state offers various opportunities for adventure tourism, including trekking, rock climbing, and water sports. The people of Kerala are known for their hospitality and warmth, making it a welcoming destination for tourists from around the world. With its beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and vibrant traditions, Kerala offers an unforgettable experience for visitors, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to India. The state continues to thrive as a model for sustainable development and environmental conservation, balancing its natural beauty with modern advancements and economic growth.
